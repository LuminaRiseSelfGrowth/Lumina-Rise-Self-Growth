The Self-Care Toolkit

Nurture your soul and recharge your spirit

What is Self-Care?

Self-care is the practice of taking action to preserve or improve one's own health, well-being, and happiness, particularly during periods of stress. It's about identifying your own needs and taking steps to meet them—it is an essential part of stress management and overall wellness.

At Lumina Rise, we believe self-care isn't selfish—it's necessary. When you prioritize your well-being, you're better equipped to show up fully in every area of your life.

Tools in This Toolkit

Our Self-Care Toolkit provides resources, practices, and guidance to help you establish a sustainable self-care routine that addresses your physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.

Daily Self-Care Practices

Simple activities you can incorporate into your daily routine to nurture yourself, including morning rituals, mindful moments, and evening wind-down practices.

Mindfulness Guides

Step-by-step instructions for various mindfulness practices, including breathing exercises, body scans, and mindful walking.

Relaxation Techniques

Methods to help you unwind and release tension, including progressive muscle relaxation, guided imagery, and stress-relief exercises.

Self-Nurturing Routines

Customizable self-care routines for morning, afternoon, and evening to help you prioritize your well-being throughout the day.

Self-Care Assessment

A comprehensive assessment to help you identify areas of self-care that need more attention and create a personalized self-care plan.

Boundary-Setting Guide

Practical advice and scripts for establishing healthy boundaries in various relationships and situations.

How to Use This Toolkit

This toolkit is designed to be flexible and adaptable to your unique needs and circumstances. We recommend starting with the Self-Care Assessment to identify which areas of self-care need the most attention in your life right now.

From there, explore the tools that resonate with you and gradually incorporate them into your routine. Remember, self-care is not about perfection—it's about progress and consistency.

As you work with these tools, pay attention to how they make you feel and adjust as needed. What works for someone else might not work for you, and what works for you today might not work for you tomorrow.
